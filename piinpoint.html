PiinPoint Website

The PiinPoint website needed an urgent revamp for our TechCrunch article release. I worked with the founders to design and build a website that represents PiinPoint.

Check out the website live

I worked as PiinPoint's lead designer during their time at YCombinator's Winter 2014 batch. I was responsible for all things design, from talking to our users to coding the front-end. Redesigning the hompage and defining the brand was a major project I worked on. I also worked on the PiinPoint map tool.

Simple graphics

I tried incorporating photos into the initial designs. Although these designs provided great visual interest, it took away from our message: Location analytics made simple.

Next, I explored a more graphical style, condensing our key features into simple pictures.

In the end, I went with a flat look with minimal colors to help the map elements stand out.

This modern style helped us stand out from our competitors' corporate websites. While large retailers loved the simplicity, this style also clicked with younger retailers, investors and potential hires visiting the website.

Coding the website

We expected to get a lot of mobile traffic from the TechCrunch article, so mobile support was a must. I wrote the HTML and added custom CSS on top of Twitter Bootstrap.

I worked on this website since it was just a landing page and PiinPoint was just getting started. It was nice to see it grow into a full fledged website and even get featured in a design blog.
